How to play golf and competition formats

Generally, competitive golf matches are played across four rounds of 18 holes each. The player who completes the entire course using the fewest shots wins. This is called the stroke play scoring format and is most widely used in golf tournaments. Often a cut is established after the first two rounds. Only the players who are above the cut mark on the leaderboard at that phase get to play the final two rounds while the remaining players drop out. In stroke play, tied rankings are acceptable but if there's a tie at the top of the leaderboard, a tie-breaker, called playoff, is played to determine a winner.
